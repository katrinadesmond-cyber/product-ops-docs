# How Product Teams Are Using Cursor to Build with AI
## Executive CPO Brief | Mar 2026

---

## 1) Executive Summary: What Matters Most
- Cursor adoption is real and broad, but maturity is uneven across teams and PLC stages.
- Teams are strongest in drafting/synthesis tasks; weakest in governance, traceability, and stage discipline.
- The winning model is not **AI as a parallel process**; it is AI embedded in the existing PLC with guardrails.
- Biggest value unlock: shift PM behavior from document production to decision velocity and evidence quality.
- Immediate priority: codify standards (prompts, checklists, automation, metrics) before scaling adoption.

---

## 2) Discovery Scope and Evidence Base
- Confluence discovery pages reviewed across Product Ops + AI playbook + Cursor blueprint (Mar 2026 exports).
- Evidence sources include PLC phase playbooks, maturity framework, operating model docs, and AI-MV artifacts.
- Signal quality: strong process evidence, early adoption patterns, and clear risk/guardrail guidance.
- Gap: limited quantified productivity baselines in current pack (instrumentation required in next phase).

---

## 3) Current Usage Pattern (Observed)
- Prompt-library usage already spans:
  - Discovery
  - PM artifacts
  - Strategy & planning
  - Analytics
  - Operations
  - GTM
- Typical current tasks:
  - Summarize customer interviews
  - Draft PRDs/user stories
  - Draft GTM and release artifacts
  - Synthesize metrics and feedback signals

---

## 4) Top-Line Themes: What Stayed the Same vs What Changed
### What stayed the same
- Outcome-led product management remains the standard.
- Continuous discovery and real customer contact remain non-negotiable.
- Human judgment owns prioritization and final decisions.
- Evidence-based decisions remain the quality bar.

### What changed
- AI is now expected in every PLC phase as a workflow accelerator.
- Teams use AI for synthesis, structure, alternatives, and consistency checks.
- Product Ops scope expands to codify prompts, quality bars, and automation rules.
- PM workflow shifts from “what doc do I write?” to “what decision am I driving?”

---

## 5) Key Outtakes: Current Challenges
- Inconsistent PLC discipline: stage-skipping and “teleporting to build” in lower-maturity teams.
- Artifact quality/linkage are uneven; teams still rebuild context from scattered/stale docs.
- RACI and decision rights are variably enforced, creating approval latency and rework loops.
- Tooling/data hygiene friction (tags, fields, cross-linking) limits AI reliability and reporting value.
- Adoption blockers are less about access and more about trust, prompt skill, and governance confidence.

---

## 6) Opportunity Areas (Next Value Pools)
- Standardize PM prompt libraries by PLC phase and artifact type.
- Add AI touchpoints directly into templates (discovery snapshot, OST, brief/spec, experiment plan, GTM).
- Deploy readiness checklists + guardrails for quality, privacy, and evidence traceability.
- Automate Jira/Confluence workflow hooks (stage tagging, artifact linkage, review triggers).
- Build one central artifact repository with naming/versioning standards and searchable history.
- Launch AI literacy program for PMs/trios/leads (intent-input-context-output practice).

---

## 7) What We Learned from Discovery
### Adoption lessons
- AI works best as a co-thinker, not a decision-maker.
- Teams gain speed first in synthesis and drafting tasks.
- Without standards, local prompt practices fragment quickly.
- Human validation checkpoints are the difference between speed and risk.

### Operating-model lessons
- Product Ops is the scaling layer for AI-enabled PLC execution.
- Maturity progression is visible: Ad-hoc → Emerging → Defined → Embedded.
- Governance must be lightweight and built into everyday flow.
- Success requires measurement of both adoption and decision quality.

---

## 8) How This Changes the PLC Workflow (Future-State)
- **Discover:** AI clusters interview evidence into snapshots/themes; PM/trio validate framing.
- **Evaluate:** AI compares opportunities and assumption gaps; trio/leadership decide trade-offs.
- **Design:** AI generates concept variants and edge cases; customers + trio test and choose.
- **Build:** AI drafts specs/stories/acceptance criteria; PM + Eng own scope/feasibility/DoD.
- **Pilot:** AI summarizes results and alternative interpretations; trio decides scale/iterate/stop.
- **Launch/Grow:** AI drafts comms and clusters adoption friction; leaders own release decisions and continuous improvement.

---

## 9) 90-Day Adoption Plan
- **Days 0–30:** Baseline maturity; select two pilot teams; define guardrails and KPIs.
- **Days 31–60:** Roll out phase-based prompt packs, template touchpoints, and workflow automations.
- **Days 61–90:** Measure cycle-time/quality deltas, codify Playbook v1, prepare scaled rollout by maturity level.
- Governance cadence: biweekly pilot review, monthly CPO checkpoint, quarterly PLC standards refresh.

---

## 10) How We Will Measure Success
- Adoption: % initiatives using standardized AI touchpoints by PLC stage
- Speed: cycle time from discovery synthesis to decision-ready artifact
- Quality: artifact readiness pass rate at stage gates
- Decision health: % decisions with explicit evidence + assumptions + trade-off log
- Rework: late-stage requirement churn/reopen rate
- Enablement: PM/trio AI literacy completion + confidence score

---

## 11) Decisions Requested from CPO
- Approve Product Ops ownership of the Cursor Playbook for PMs (standards + governance).
- Approve 90-day pilot with two product teams and explicit success gates.
- Mandate PLC-stage traceability (artifact links + decision logs) as rollout prerequisite.
- Sponsor cross-functional enablement and monthly executive review cadence.
