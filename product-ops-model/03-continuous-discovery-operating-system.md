# 03. Continuous Discovery Operating System

## Objective

Create a repeatable discovery system that is lightweight enough for delivery teams and robust enough for strategic decision making.

## Discovery operating loop

1. **Collect signals**
   - Customer interviews.
   - Product analytics and funnel behavior.
   - Support tickets and sales feedback.
   - AI-assisted pattern detection in qualitative/quantitative inputs.

2. **Synthesize insights**
   - Cluster pain points and unmet needs.
   - Separate facts, assumptions, and interpretations.
   - Estimate opportunity size and urgency.

3. **Prioritize opportunities**
   - Score by customer impact, business value, strategic fit, confidence.
   - Document tradeoffs and dependencies.

4. **Test assumptions**
   - Run experiments at the smallest useful scope.
   - Define expected signal, threshold, and timebox.

5. **Decide and adapt**
   - Continue, pivot, pause, or stop.
   - Update roadmap and backlog based on evidence.

## Team-level discovery rhythm

### Weekly minimum
- 1 customer evidence touchpoint (interview, observation, or analysis).
- 1 synthesis checkpoint (30-45 minutes).
- 1 experiment or assumption update.
- 1 "show-your-work" update linking evidence -> assumptions -> decisions.

### Bi-weekly minimum
- Opportunity-Solution Tree update.
- Stakeholder evidence review.
- Prioritization refresh based on new learnings.

## Portfolio-level discovery rhythm

### Monthly
- Product Ops publishes cross-team discovery themes:
  - repeated customer problems;
  - high-confidence opportunities;
  - failed assumptions and implications.

### Quarterly
- Portfolio discovery health review:
  - evidence recency and volume;
  - experiment throughput;
  - decision velocity;
  - impact realized from discovery-driven decisions.

## Discovery metrics

Track these indicators:

### Input metrics
- Number of customer touchpoints per team per month.
- Coverage across target segments/personas.
- Evidence recency (days since last customer signal).

### Process metrics
- Number of assumptions documented and tested.
- Experiment cycle time (idea to decision).
- Decision latency (evidence available to decision made).

### Outcome metrics
- Percent of roadmap items linked to validated opportunities.
- Change in target product KPI(s).
- Reduction in rework from late-stage learning.

## AI usage guardrails in discovery

### Allowed and encouraged
- Transcript summarization.
- Theme clustering and tagging.
- Drafting interview guides.
- Suggesting follow-up questions.

### Requires additional review
- Persona generation without real evidence.
- Auto-prioritization without explicit criteria.
- Causal claims from correlation-only data.

### Prohibited
- Fabricating user quotes or evidence.
- Using sensitive data in unapproved AI tools.
- Presenting AI output as validated fact without source checks.

## Discovery quality rescue triggers

When these appear, teams should pause commitment and run a discovery reset:
- stale or weak evidence for active initiatives;
- solution direction chosen before problem clarity;
- unresolved assumptions treated as facts;
- stakeholder debate driven by opinion rank rather than evidence.

## Discovery roles and responsibilities

- **PM**: owns opportunity framing and decision quality.
- **Design/Research**: leads evidence collection and synthesis quality.
- **Engineering/Data**: validates technical feasibility and instrumentation.
- **Product Ops**: maintains standards, tooling, and cross-team insights.

## Definition of done for discovery

Discovery for an initiative is "done enough to commit" when:
- opportunity is clearly defined and evidence-backed;
- key assumptions are tested or explicitly risk-accepted;
- expected impact is measurable;
- next delivery step is low ambiguity for the team.
