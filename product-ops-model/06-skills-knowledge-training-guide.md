# 06. Skills Knowledge Training Guide (Shape Up PLC + AI Product Ops)

## Purpose

Provide a practical, role-based training program so teams can execute the current Shape Up PLC with consistent quality, speed, and responsible AI practices.

## Training outcomes

By the end of training, teams should be able to:
- convert raw customer signals into structured insights;
- frame opportunities with measurable outcomes;
- shape initiatives using appetite (fixed time, variable scope);
- produce execution-ready one-pagers and PRD-lite artifacts;
- run release and learning loops with clear ownership;
- use AI as an accelerator without compromising evidence quality.

## Audience and target proficiency

| Role | Target Level | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Product Manager | Advanced | Owns opportunity, shaping, and decision quality |
| Product Designer / Research | Practitioner-Advanced | Owns evidence quality and synthesis |
| Engineering Lead | Practitioner | Owns feasibility, risk, and delivery reliability |
| Data/Analytics | Practitioner | Owns measurement and experiment instrumentation |
| Product Ops | Advanced | Owns standards, coaching, and governance |
| GTM / Customer teams | Beginner-Practitioner | Contribute messaging, readiness, and feedback loops |

## Curriculum design (8 modules)

### Module 1: Customer Insight Capture (Canny)
- **Objective**: create high-quality insights with required tags and evidence.
- **Artifacts**: `template-canny-customer-insight-capture.md`.
- **Practice**: convert 10 raw interview notes into tagged, evidence-backed insights.
- **Pass criteria**:
  - all required tags present,
  - evidence source included,
  - no solutioning in signal capture.

### Module 2: Opportunity Assessment
- **Objective**: frame opportunities with measurable outcomes and assumptions.
- **Artifacts**: `template-opportunity-assessment.md`.
- **Practice**: produce one opportunity assessment from 3+ insights.
- **Pass criteria**:
  - clear problem statement,
  - KPI baseline and target present,
  - top assumptions and confidence captured.

### Module 3: Shape Up Pitch One-Pager
- **Objective**: shape work into fixed-time, variable-scope proposals.
- **Artifacts**: `template-shape-up-pitch-one-pager.md`.
- **Practice**: draft a pitch including appetite, out-of-scope, and rabbit holes.
- **Pass criteria**:
  - appetite is explicit,
  - must-have vs nice-to-have is clear,
  - major risks and mitigations are realistic.

### Module 4: Execution Readiness (PRD-lite)
- **Objective**: create implementation-ready documentation.
- **Artifacts**: `template-prd-lite.md`.
- **Practice**: convert accepted pitch into PRD-lite with rollout and guardrails.
- **Pass criteria**:
  - requirements are unambiguous,
  - success and guardrail metrics defined,
  - launch/rollback criteria included.

### Module 5: GTM + Release Excellence
- **Objective**: operationalize launch, release, and communication plans.
- **Artifacts**: existing GTM and release templates in current Confluence process.
- **Practice**: run a mock go/no-go review with dependencies and escalation path.
- **Pass criteria**:
  - readiness checklist complete,
  - support/hypercare plan defined,
  - ownership and contacts clear.

### Module 6: AI-Assisted Authoring with Integrity
- **Objective**: use AI to accelerate drafting and synthesis safely.
- **Artifacts**: `.cursor/rules/product-ops-rules.mdc`, `skills-catalog.md`.
- **Practice**: improve an artifact draft using AI while preserving source traceability.
- **Pass criteria**:
  - all claims source-linked,
  - assumptions clearly marked,
  - no fabricated evidence or quotes.

### Module 7: Rules vs Skills Governance
- **Objective**: help teams apply non-negotiable rules while reusing skills effectively.
- **Artifacts**: `08-cursor-rules-and-skills-governance.md`.
- **Practice**: classify 20 examples as rule vs skill and define ownership.
- **Pass criteria**:
  - classification is correct and justified,
  - owner and review cadence identified,
  - ambiguous guidance converted into explicit rule/skill format.

### Module 8: Design-to-Dev Workflow Excellence
- **Objective**: reduce manual handoff and improve design system reuse quality.
- **Artifacts**:
  - `template-product-design-decision-record.md`,
  - `template-plc-checkpoint-workflow.md`.
- **Practice**: run a mock UI initiative through checkpoints with decision record.
- **Pass criteria**:
  - reuse-first design decision is explicit,
  - checkpoint evidence is complete,
  - handoff clarity issues are identified and mitigated.

## 4-week rollout plan

### Week 1
- Training kickoff and baseline assessment.
- Module 1 + Module 2 workshops.

### Week 2
- Module 3 workshop (Shape Up pitch and appetite).
- Coaching office hours on real initiatives.

### Week 3
- Module 4 + Module 5 workshops.
- Mock go/no-go review simulation.

### Week 4
- Module 6 + Module 7 workshops.
- Module 8 capstone simulation and final assessment.
- Team-level improvement plans and certification.

## Assessment rubric (simple and scalable)

| Criterion | 0 = Missing | 1 = Partial | 2 = Strong |
|---|---|---|---|
| Evidence integrity | No source evidence | Some evidence, weak traceability | Clear source-backed evidence |
| Outcome clarity | No KPI link | KPI present, weak target | KPI baseline + target + owner |
| Scope discipline | Scope unclear | Appetite present, weak tradeoffs | Appetite + out-of-scope + tradeoffs |
| Risk quality | Risks missing | Risks listed, weak mitigation | Risks prioritized and mitigated |
| Learning loop | No follow-up plan | Follow-up vague | Clear decision loop and next step |

## Operating model for training maintenance

- **Owner**: Product Operations.
- **Review cadence**: monthly update, quarterly major refresh.
- **Inputs for updates**:
  - common quality failures in artifacts,
  - launch retrospectives,
  - evolving AI policy/compliance requirements.
- **Success indicators**:
  - increased artifact pass rates,
  - faster decision cycles,
  - reduced rework,
  - stronger confidence in roadmap decisions.
