We've updated the Pitch One‑Pager to better support the shift we’re making toward a delivery‑led execution model for the upcoming cycle. Moving to a leaner approach this will helps us stay aligned, reduce overhead, and keep clarity on what each team is delivering and why.

The one‑pager is designed to:

- Provide clear direction, focus on execution clarity, dependencies and scope
- Maintain value and intent without the heaviness of full opportunity shaping

**Background & Context**

Provide enough relevant context and background so teams understand where this work fits in the UNL delivery program

**Problem Statement** *(What This Feature Solves at a Practical Level)*

Describe the user or business need this feature addresses, focusing on clarity, not so much the discovery

What user problem or business gap does this feature close?

**Delivery‑Focused Feature Summary** *(What You Are Building)*

A concise, execution-ready outline of the proposed implementation:

- Core functionality required
- What is fixed / pre‑defined
- What is flexible or still being designed
- What decisions your team owns vs. shared decisions with other teams

**Appetite - Fixed Time, Variable Scope**

Given capacity and cross‑team delivery constraints:

- What is **must‑deliver** for Q1
- What is **nice-to-have** but not required
- What is **explicitly out of scope**
- What external constraints (APIs, platform readiness, sequencing) apply
- What other teams must deliver before/with/after you

**Execution Assumptions & Dependencies** *(What Needs to Be True to Ship This)*

Let other teams understand what your team is counting on and what they may need to support or unblock

Communicate the assumptions your delivery hinges on:

- Technical assumptions
- Resourcing assumptions (squad capacity, availability of design/engineering)
- Dependencies on other Q1 features, teams, or shared services
- Early validation or discovery used to reduce risk

**Measures of Success**

Ensure every team understands what “done” means for your feature and how success will be confirmed

Shift from “big outcome metrics” to delivery-appropriate indicators:

- Acceptance criteria (functional, UX, technical)
- Quality and performance expectations
- Customer or internal validation steps
- Any value signals visible soon after release (usage, enablement, reduction of defects, etc.)

**Potential Risks Associated with the Solution** *(What Could Delay or Complicate Delivery)*

Prevent surprises and encourage proactive mitigation across the program

Explain how you identified and eliminated potential distractions, complexities, or uncertainties that could derail the team's focus during the development cycle

**Gain Alignment**

Reinforce shared understanding in a multi‑team environment and make sure everyone knows where their work intersects

Finish by reaffirming:

- What your team will deliver
- What is in/out of scope
- Key dependencies and handover points
- Important dates/milestones
- Who needs to align (tech leads, design, PMs, PlatOps, or other squads)

## Pitch Check List

Review the checklist below to ensure you addressed the key considerations above.

- **Help Them See It** - Used simple sketches, wireframes, or diagrams to help visualise the proposed solution
- **Show your work** - Identified what insights you will use? Include any data, customer feedback, or market trends that underscore the importance of addressing this specific opportunity
- **Select Customer Segment** - For whom are we solving this problem? Which customers are affected?
- **Experiments** - Vet a few different routes on the solution front, considering proposing experiment(s) as a part of your pitch
- **Prioritisation and Trade-Offs -** Discussed any trade-offs made in determining the scope and solution, explaining why certain features were prioritised over others to stay within the appetite
- **Assumptions and Risks -** Explained what assumptions did you test to evaluate your solutions?
- **Define Measures of Success** - What metrics will be used to measure the success of the opportunities? Providing a clear benchmark for success, what’s that baseline?
- **Out of Scope** - What is not in the scope of the proposed solution? Always good to call out boundaries of what you are not doing.
- **Go To Market** - How will you get your solution to the customer?
