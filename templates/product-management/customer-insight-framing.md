## Why Canny (for Insights)

Canny is our single source of truth for customer problems, needs, and emerging signals.
We use it to:

- Capture feedback consistently
- Make patterns visible across customers and time
- Ground product decisions in real evidence
- Support prioritisation, pitching, and exec storytelling

## When to Capture an Insight

Create a Canny post when feedback comes from:

- Customer interviews or discovery sessions
- Sales, retailer, or support escalations
- Workshops or field research
- Repeated anecdotal feedback worth validating

If it’s specific, impactful, or repeated, it belongs in Canny.

## Where Insights Live (Boards)

Boards reflect maturity, not teams.

- Customer Problems (Validated)
  Repeated, evidenced problems
- Emerging Needs & Signals
  Early patterns, weak signals
- Friction & Blockers
  Issues blocking spend, launch, or scale
- Opportunities (Not Yet Shaped)
  Framed problems ready for exploration

## How to Create a High-Quality Insight

### 1. Post Title (standardised)

Format:
[Problem] + [Who] + [Impact]

Example:
Advertisers can’t forecast spend → leads to under-delivery

### *2. Post Description (required)*

```
Customer / Requestor:
- Woolworths (Sponsored Search Advertiser)

Context:
- Customer interview – Jan 2026
- Managing always-on campaigns

Problem / Need:
- No visibility into demand before launch

Why it matters:
- Causes under-spend and low confidence in scaling budgets

Evidence:
- “We don’t know if we’ll hit spend until the campaign is live.”
```

### 3. Apply Required Tags (Non-Negotiable)

Every post must include:

- Customer: Customer: Woolworths
- Role: Role: Advertiser
- Theme: Theme: Ad Serving
- Keyword(s): Keyword: forecasting, Keyword: budget pacing
- Time: When: H1 2026

Tags enable search, clustering, and exec-level synthesis.

## How We Use Tags

Tags are structured and intentional:

- Customers → attribution & segmentation
- Themes → strategy alignment
- Keywords → pattern detection
- Time → trend analysis

Avoid free-text or one-off tags.

## From Signal → Insight → Opportunity

We separate learning from solutioning.

- Signal: Single piece of feedback
- Insight: Repeated pattern with impact
  (Note this in a comment: “Mentioned by 8/12 advertisers”)
- Opportunity: Framed problem ready for exploration
  (Created later — not in the initial post)

## How This Feeds Decision-Making

Canny insights directly support:

- Quarterly planning & prioritisation
- Shape Up pitches
- Opportunity sizing
- Exec readouts & strategy reviews

If it’s not in Canny, it’s anecdote — not evidence.
