# Pitch Template: Identity Everywhere — Evidence Engine Initiative

## 0) Executive Prioritisation (What to communicate first)

1. **Business risk and urgency (Why now):** Identity Everywhere is a strategic lever, but adoption is stalled at **9/101 retailers (10%)**, limiting measurement, personalisation, and monetisation outcomes.
2. **Core initiative value:** The **Evidence Engine** turns identity from a perceived black box into a measurable, client-facing asset (coverage, match rate, enrichment, and ROI signals).
3. **Commercial outcome:** Use proof-first transparency to accelerate CoreID and tagging adoption, reduce legal friction, and strengthen renewal/upsell confidence.
4. **Pilot decision required:** Approve a **12-week pilot** with priority emerging-market retailers to validate adoption lift and cycle-time reduction.
5. **Scale path:** If pilot KPIs are met, operationalise as a reusable global model for sales, onboarding, legal, and product teams.

---

## 1) Background & Context

Identity Everywhere is a strategic enabler for revenue growth, closed-loop measurement, and omnichannel personalisation. Current adoption is fragmented, and that fragmentation is weakening both GTM confidence and platform differentiation.

From the 2026 strategy pack and discovery synthesis:
- CoreID is often discussed as a technical feature rather than a business enabler.
- Legal/privacy objections slow onboarding and contract progression.
- Retailers and internal teams repeatedly ask for tangible ROI proof before committing.
- Emerging markets (especially APAC/EU) need localised, lower-friction implementation and clearer value narratives.

The proposed opportunity is to launch an **Evidence Engine** capability that visibly tracks identity progress and ties it to business outcomes.

## 2) Problem Statement

Retailers, legal stakeholders, and client-facing teams do not have a trusted, consistent way to see whether identity adoption is improving campaign outcomes. This creates a trust gap:

- Identity is perceived as complex and risky.
- Legal/compliance concerns dominate commercial discussions.
- Proof of value is inconsistent and often arrives too late.
- Onboarding slows, adoption remains low, and growth opportunities are delayed.

**Customers/segments affected:**
- Retailers evaluating CoreID/tagging adoption (especially emerging and mid-market)
- Sales, Client Success, Onboarding, and Legal teams requiring credible proof for decision-making
- Executive stakeholders responsible for adoption, revenue growth, and market expansion

### Show your work (evidence and insights used)

- 14 Identity Everywhere interviews and discovery outputs indicate recurring needs: legal simplification, outcome-led messaging, and proof-point delivery.
- Strategy pack highlights: “identity is the enabler, not the product” and calls out Evidence Engine dashboards as a key pilot initiative.
- Customer Insight Framing guidance reinforces traceability and synthesis discipline:
  - Capture structured evidence (who, context, impact, quote)
  - Tag by customer/theme/time for pattern detection
  - Separate signal -> insight -> opportunity for executive-quality decisioning

## 3) Outline Solution

Deliver a new **Evidence Engine** initiative with three integrated components:

1. **Client-Facing Identity Coverage Dashboard (MVP)**
   - Identity coverage trend (site/session/customer/CoreID progression)
   - Match rate trend (online/offline where available)
   - Enrichment depth indicators
   - Onboarding stage/health and readiness status

2. **Proof-of-Value Layer**
   - Standardised before/after ROI views
   - “Wasted spend vs. uplift potential” scenario views
   - Reusable case-study outputs for GTM and renewal conversations

3. **Operational Evidence Backbone**
   - Structured insight capture and tagging inputs (aligned to Customer Insight Framing)
   - Standard cadence for product + GTM + legal review
   - Executive readout format for adoption and risk signals

### What this enables

- Trust through transparency
- Faster legal and onboarding progression
- Better GTM storytelling with measurable proof
- Stronger prioritisation on where to invest next (markets, retailers, capabilities)

## 4) Appetite — Fixed Time, Variable Scope

**Pilot appetite:** 12 weeks (kickoff initiative for stakeholder approval)

### Must deliver (in scope)
- Evidence Engine dashboard v1 for pilot retailers
- Standard KPI definitions and data contracts
- Monthly executive evidence readout
- GTM-ready proof-point pack template

### Nice to have (if capacity allows)
- Automated ROI scenario modelling
- Regional benchmark comparisons
- Alerting for match-rate decay or onboarding risk

### Out of scope (pilot)
- Full global rollout
- Complete household-level attribution in all regions
- Deep automation across every integration method on day one

## 5) Key Assumptions (Justify the approach)

1. **Transparency reduces friction:** If retailers/legal teams can see identity progress and controls, compliance objections and delays reduce.
2. **Proof drives adoption:** Tangible, consistent proof points increase conversion, onboarding momentum, and renewal confidence.
3. **Pilot-first works:** A focused pilot in priority markets can produce reusable playbooks faster than broad rollout.
4. **Outcome-led messaging performs better:** Positioning identity as a revenue/measurement enabler improves stakeholder engagement.

## 6) Measures of Success

### Baseline (current)
- CoreID/tagging adoption: **10% (9/101 retailers)**.
- Inconsistent proof artifacts and variable readiness visibility.

### Pilot success metrics (12 weeks)
- **100% of pilot retailers** visible in dashboard with agreed KPI definitions.
- **>=20% reduction** in onboarding cycle time for pilot cohort.
- **>=20% reduction** in legal/compliance turnaround for pilot cohort.
- **Match-rate uplift trend established** for pilot retailers (target to be finalised per retailer baseline).
- **Proof-point output cadence live:** at least 1 executive-ready case narrative per pilot retailer.

### Strategic trajectory (12 months, program-level)
- Adoption progression aligned to strategy target range (**40–60%** where feasible by segment/market maturity).
- Higher GTM conversion and stronger renewal/upsell confidence linked to proof-of-value delivery.

## 7) Potential Risks Associated with the Solution (Rabbit holes)

1. **Data quality / instrumentation inconsistency**
   - Mitigation: KPI dictionary, ingestion QA checks, and onboarding data readiness gate.
2. **Over-engineering dashboard before proving value**
   - Mitigation: strict MVP scope and weekly scope governance.
3. **Privacy/compliance concerns with visibility design**
   - Mitigation: legal-by-design review before pilot launch; no exposure of disallowed data.
4. **Cross-team ownership ambiguity**
   - Mitigation: explicit RACI and weekly decision forum.
5. **Executive narrative drift into technical detail**
   - Mitigation: standard “outcome-first” readout template (revenue, risk, speed, confidence).

## 8) Gain Alignment (Stakeholder approval ask)

### Decision requested
Approve launch of the **Evidence Engine Pilot Initiative** for 12 weeks as a priority Identity Everywhere kickoff project.

### Required commitments
- Product + Data + Engineering capacity for dashboard v1
- GTM and Client Success support for proof-point packaging
- Legal/compliance design partnership for privacy-safe rollout
- Executive sponsor for monthly evidence checkpoints

### Kickoff milestones
- **Week 1-2:** Confirm pilot retailers, KPI definitions, data readiness checklist
- **Week 3-6:** Build dashboard v1 + proof-point template
- **Week 7-10:** Run pilot readouts, collect adoption/friction data
- **Week 11-12:** Executive review, go/no-go for scale phase
